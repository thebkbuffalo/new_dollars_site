About

The Bottom Dollars​ were formed in the basement studios of Brooklyn’s Cameo Gallery in 2010 by Brian “Cherch” Cherchiglia and Evan “Buffalo” Berg. The group leapt onto the radar with the release of ​The Halcyon Days [EP].​ Anchored by Chris Urriola (bass) & Sean Spada (keyboards), the Dollars toured heavily in their early years, quickly garnering praise for their jaw-dropping live performances from press outlets such as ​NPR, Paste Magazine, Village Voice, a​ nd​ Daytrotter (​where they’ve logged an impressive four live sessions).

With a massive sound built on top of a lockdown rhythm section, these “serial heart stealers” (​The Deli Magazine​) made their home after midnight, often hosting late-night sets (​“30 Minute Set? Pfft. The Bottom Dollars go HARD. 90 Minutes or All Night”​ ​Village Voice) ​where they’d unabashedly flex their debaucherous charm, carrying sweaty, sold-out New York City crowds deep into the early morning with their unique brand of rock & roll.

In 2014, the Dollars released their debut LP ​Meet Me In Cognito,​ mixed by John Siket (​Sonic Youth,​ ​The Replacements,​ ​Phish)​ , which catapulted the band to new heights: including a ​month-long residency at Brooklyn Bowl​, SXSW, and bigger tours, culminating with a ​two-set finalé at the Bowery Ballroom​. Cherch and Berg relocated to Los Angeles, recruiting multi-instrumentalist Mick Greenwood (​The Self-Proclaimed Rockstars, Zerobot​) and returned to the road, actively canvassing the western United States. They released a ​limited edition 7”​: “​21st Century, Man!”​ for Record Store Day in 2017.

Shortly after, The Bottom Dollars amicably agreed to an indefinite hiatus.

Cherch returned to Brooklyn, performing again with Chris, Sean and drummer/guitarist Jeremy Kolker (The Shacks, God Tiny)​. Berg relocated to Chicago, while Greenwood remained in LA, taking the opportunity to work alongside Billy Corgan (​The Smashing Pumpkins,​ ​National Wrestling Alliance​) in the world of professional wrestling.

In the spring of 2019, producer Tim O’Sullivan (​Yeah Yeah Yeahs, Childish Gambino, Anderson Paak​) suggested that the band reunite to record an album with him at the famed Barefoot Recording studios in Hollywood, CA. All six musicians agreed and approached the record together as multi-instrumentalists. Berg requested that the new album carry a message of social consciousness, and Cherch obliged; composing the material for ​Marvelous (​ LP, release date TBA) in just a few short weeks. ​Marvelous guides the listener through the disturbing realities of our present, contemplating voyeurism, dystopia, oppression, social inequalities and the widespread discomfort that seems to be enveloping so many of us.

With their new album on the way, the band is also returning to live performance with the same multi-instrumental approach they took to tracking. And with an arsenal of tube-driven guitars, two drum sets, analog, digital and modular synthesis, and six musicians, the nights are about to get louder and they’re about to get longer: The Bottom Dollars are back.
